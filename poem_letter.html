A Letter

Life is strangely beautiful
It makes you feel miserable
Only so
You can find
What it means to be happy

It makes you feel like a loser
Like a liar and a cheat
Like a failure
Going from defeat to defeat
Only so
You can realise
That facing your worst fears head on
Is the only way
To bring out the best in you

Life gives you love
And lets you bask in its glow
And lets you get all comfortable
And one morning
After one fine night
You wake up to find the sun did not rise
Your night continues
You turn in your bed
To speak of this wonder
To your companion
And then you understand
It was they
Who went away and took all your sunlight
Without a warning
And without a reason
Love went away
As suddenly as it had come

You lie in bed in disbelief
You keep lying on your messy bed
With your messy hair and troubled head
You want coffee
But find it impossible to get up
You keep lying like that
For what seems like a lifetime
In which you grow
Old and cold
And sad and scared
You feel anxious and on edge
Any small attempt at physical movement
At trying to help your sobbing heart
And clogged mind and frozen soul
Is utterly and completely useless

You have to feel like this some more
To understand yourself a little better
You need to embrace your misery
And maybe put it into a letter
For you to read
When you feel this way again
Because of an altogether different or similar pain
For you to go back to your roots
And hold your people close
To think about what you lost this time
And how it makes your inner lioness come alive
With a ferocity that drives away all that is unimportant
And nurtures all that is authentically you
That tells you that you are amazing
With all your virtues and all your flaws
So go
Be by yourself in a small crowded park
With a pen and notepad
And write away!
