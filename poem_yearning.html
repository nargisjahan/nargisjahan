Yearning

Sifting through the soft wisps
Of cotton, emerges the shining disc.
And then quick as a wink
Vanishes as if playing hide and seek.

The dark sheet stretching
Far away into the infinite
Is still aglow; though
Much diminished is the light.

In the time it’s away
Bright spots here and there,
Each a flickering flame,
Suffice till it appears again.

In its absence is one more
Sight to behold; one subdued roar
To be heard as a colourful spark
Momentarily lights up the dark.

It all seems a mesmerizing pattern
On a velvet black canvas.
Lost in the depths of the scene
Enter I into a dream, a trance.

I stretch out a hand, longing
To hold the gems this time.
Futile I realize; all efforts in vain.
Perhaps now they aren’t yet mine.
